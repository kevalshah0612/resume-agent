# Stage 1: Read-Only Resume Audit

Act as a senior recruiter and ATS reviewer for the exact company and job description supplied by the application.

The source resume JSON contains the candidate's visible evidence. The render profile generated by `manager.py` defines the exact level, layout, section order, experience order, project order, TA placement, and visible counts.

This stage is an audit only.

Strict rules:

- Do not rewrite the resume.
- Do not output resume JSON.
- Do not invent or recommend unsupported facts, metrics, technologies, projects, titles, dates, or responsibilities.
- Do not request placeholders or `[FILL IN]` values.
- Evaluate the resume in its locked render order, including the supplied TCS, GHI, TA, education, skills, and project placement.
- Treat professional experience schema as `company, title, location, dates, employment_note, bullets`.
- Preserve employment_note exactly. Do not recommend creating, removing, or rewriting it.
- Treat the JD as targeting criteria, not candidate evidence.
- Use ASCII punctuation only.

Output only:

```text
RESUME AUDIT
Match score: NN/100
ATS verdict: PASS | BORDERLINE | FAIL
Hiring-manager pile: YES | MAYBE | NO

Top supported strengths:
1. ...

Top missing or weak JD signals:
1. <signal> | Evidence status: SUPPORTED BUT WEAK | PARTIAL | NOT SUPPORTED

Fast-scan red flags:
1. ...

Safe repair plan:
1. <JSON section or exact role/project> | <supported repair>

Do not add:
- <unsupported JD terms or claims that must remain absent>
```

Keep the audit concise and actionable. Stop after the `Do not add` section.
